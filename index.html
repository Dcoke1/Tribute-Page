Timeline of events leading to Travis Scott's fame

  • Born Jacques Webster in a suburb of Houston, Travis Scott began making music as a teenager

  • He formed a duo named "the Graduates" and produced an EP in 2009 and subsequently two full length albums for a group called "the Classmates" in 2011.

  • After dropping out of college Travis moved to Los Angeles and began recording music on his own. He Met T.I and Kanye West and was hired as an in-house producer for G.O.O.D Music and appeared on CRUEL SUMMER in 2012

  • In 2013 Travis Scott released his debut mixtape "Owl Pharaoh" nominated by BET Music Awards for best mixtape

  • The buildup to Travis's first studio album "Rodeo" was preceeded by a March 2015 US Rodeo tour for which Travis headlined, sold out many dates

  • Rodeo including the song Antidote which peaked at #16 on Billboard Top 100 list. It was Travis's first platinum single

  • Travis's extended his commercial presence with featured spots on Wiz Khalifa's "Bake Sale," Rihanna's "Woo," and Kanye West's "FML," as well as a collaboration with Young Thug and Quavo, "Pick Up the Phone," a midyear hit issued as the lead single of Young Thug's JEFFERY.

  • 2016 featured Travis's 2nd studio album, Birds in the Trap Sing McKnight, a woozy, mostly midtempo set. It went straight to the top of the Billboard 200.

  • Leading up to his third LP, Travis was featured on a slew of tracks, including platinum singles headlined by SZA ("Love Galore"), Drake ("Portland"), Miguel ("Sky Walker"), 2 Chainz ("4 AM)," and Trippie Redd ("Dark Knight Dummo"). Travis's own "Butterfly Effect" arrived during this torrent of hits in May 2017 and promptly went platinum as well.

  • Seven months later, Huncho Jack, Jack Huncho, a collaborative full-length with Quavo, entered the Billboard 200 at number three.

  • Astroworld, which he considered the true follow-up to Rodeo. Featuring "Butterfly Effect," the album -- named after a demolished Houston amusement park -- debuted at number one and was supported with a second single, "Sicko Mode."

  • Travis Scott performed at SuperBowl LIII February 3, 2019
